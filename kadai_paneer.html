Kadai Paneer

Description

Kadai Paneer is a bold, restaurant-favourite North Indian dish known for its thick, spicy tomato-based gravy and crunchy bell peppers.
The secret to its signature flavour is the Kadai Masala, a freshly ground spice blend.

Ingredients

  • 250g paneer (cubed)
  • 1 large capsicum/bell pepper (diced)
  • 1 medium onion (cubed/petals separated)
  • 2 finely chopped onions
  • 2-3 tomatoes (pureed or finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Kadai Masala Spices: 2 tbsp coriander seeds, 1 tsp fennel seeds, 1 tsp cumin seeds, 3-4 dried Kashmiri red chillies, and 5-6 peppercorns
  • 1 tsp Kasuri Methi (dried fenugreek)
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp fresh cream (optional)
  • Ginger juliennes

Steps to be Followed

  1. Prepare Kadai Masala:

    Dry roast the coriander seeds, fennel, cumin, dried chillies, and peppercorns until fragrant. Cool and grind into a coarse powder.

  2. Sauté Veggies:

    Heat 1 tbsp oil in a Kadhai (wok). Sauté the cubed onions and capsicum on high heat for 2-3 minutes until slightly charred but still crunchy. Remove and set aside.

  3. Make the Gravy:

    Heat more oil/ghee in the same pan. Sauté chopped onions until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.

  4. Simmer Masala:

    Add tomatoes and salt. Cook until soft and oil starts to separate. Stir in the prepared Kadai Masala, turmeric, and red chilli powder.

  5. Assemble:

    Add about ½ cup of water to reach your desired consistency. Add the sautéed veggies and paneer cubes. Mix gently so the paneer doesn't break.

  6. Garnish

    Finish with Kasuri Methi (crushed between palms), cream (for a richer texture), and fresh coriander.Finish with Kasuri Methi (crushed between palms), cream (for a richer texture), and fresh coriander.
