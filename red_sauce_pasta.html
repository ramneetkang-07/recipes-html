Red Sauce Pasta

Home

Description

This recipe for Red Sauce Pasta produces a tangy, vibrant, and satisfying dish in under 30 minutes, utilizing fresh tomatoes and pantry staples. It is highly customizable, allowing for added vegetables or cheese.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups (120-150g) Pasta
  • 4 large or 5 medium ripe tomatoes (blanched and pureed)
  • 1 tablespoon, finely chopped garlic
  • ½ cup, finely chopped (or shallots) onion
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1-2 tablespoons Tomato Ketchup/Sauce (to balance acidity)
  • ½ tsp chili flakes, ½ tsp mixed herbs (oregano/basil), ¼ tsp black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • (Optional) 1 tsp sugar (if tomatoes are too sour)
  • (Optional) Grated parmesan/processed cheese for garnish

Steps to be Followed

  1. Boil the Pasta:

    Bring 4-6 cups of water to a rolling boil, add 1 tsp salt, and cook pasta until al dente (firm to the bite). Reserve ½ cup of pasta water, then drain the pasta.

  2. Prepare Tomato Puree:

    To get a smooth sauce, blanch 4-5 tomatoes (boil for 5 mins, peel skin) and blend them into a smooth puree.

  3. Sauté Aromatics:

    Heat olive oil in a large pan or kadai. Add chopped garlic and sauté until fragrant. Add the onions and sauté until translucent/light golden.

  4. Cook the Sauce:

    Add the tomato puree, red chili flakes, mixed herbs, black pepper, and salt. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the raw smell of the tomatoes leaves and the sauce thickens.

  5. Add Condiments:

    Stir in the tomato ketchup/sauce and mix well.

  6. Combine:

    Add the cooked pasta to the sauce. If the sauce is too thick, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to make it creamy and help it coat the pasta.

  7. Finish & Serve:

    Toss for 1-2 minutes on high heat. Garnish with cheese and fresh herbs, and serve hot.
