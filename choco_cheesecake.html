Eggless Chocolate Cheesecake

Home

Description

This chocolate version is baked in a thick Oreo crust and garnished with fresh chocolate whipped cream for a truly epic ending to your meal!
It has a luscious and creamy texture. No cracks and this cheesecake DOES NOT sink in the center!
This is an easy, fully make-ahead dessert, that everyone LOVES!

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups (200 g) Oreo crumbs (about 24-26 Oreos)
  • 3 tablespoons (42 g) butter, melted
  • 3 tablespoons (30 g) cornstarch
  • 3 tablespoons (45 ml) water
  • ¾ cup (180 ml) heavy cream
  • 1 - 1 ½ cups (180 - 270 g) semi-sweet chocolate
  • 1 teaspoon instant espresso powder
  • 678 g full fat cream cheese
  • ½ cup (100 g) granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup (28 g) natural unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 397 g sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Steps to be followed

  1. Make the Crust

    1. Preheat oven to 350º F (180º C). Lightly grease 9-inch round springform pan.
    2. In a medium mixing bowl, combine the Oreo crumbs and melted butter. Pat the mixture firmly into the bottom of the prepared pan.
    3. Bake the crust for 10 minutes. Set the pan on a wire rack and cool the crust completely. Leave the oven on.

  2. Make the Filling

    1. Mix cornstarch and water in a small bowl until smooth. Set aside.
    2. Combine the heavy cream and chopped chocolate in a small microwave-safe bowl or cup. Heat in 30-second bursts, stirring after each burst until the chocolate has melted, and the mixture is smooth. Stir in the espresso powder and set the mixture aside.
    3. Place the cream cheese in the bowl of an electric mixer, beat on low speed for 2-3 minutes until smooth and free of any lumps. Add sugar and cocoa powder; continue mixing until incorporated. Gradually add sweetened condensed milk and beat until creamy, for 1-2 minutes. Add vanilla extract and cornstarch mixture; beat until well incorporated.
    4. Add melted chocolate mixture; beat until well incorporated.
    5. Pour the mixture over the cooled crust; tap gently to remove any air bubbles.

  3. Prepare The Pan and Water Bath

    Place the cheesecake springform pan on MULTIPLE LAYERS of heavy-duty aluminum foil and fold up the sides around it (this is to keep the water from seeping into the cheesecake). Place the springform pan in a large roasting pan. Pour hot water into the roasting pan until the water is about halfway up the sides of the cheesecake pan.

  4. Bake

    1. Bake for 40 minutes or until the edges are just barely puffed. The center of the cheesecake should still wobble when you remove it from the oven. Turn the oven off and leave the cheesecake inside the oven for another 40 minutes (with the door closed). Remove the cheesecake from the oven and set it on a cooling rack to finish cooling. After 15 minutes on a wire rack, carefully run a knife between the cheesecake and the inside of the pan. Don't remove the sides of the pan yet.
    2. When the cheesecake is completely cool at room temperature, cover it with foil or plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 6 hours (overnight better).
    3. When ready to serve, loosen the cheesecake from the sides of the pan by running a thin metal spatula around the inside rim. Unmold and transfer to a serving plate.

  5. Decorate

    You can decorate your cheesecake whatever your heart desires.
